About Me

👨‍🔬

I am a PhD researcher studying pulsars and FRBs. My expertise lies in developing pipelines and conducting radio follow-up and survey to search for pulsar and FRB signatures.

Currently at the French National Centre for Scientific Research. I work with radio single dishes and interferometers around the world to observe pulsars and hunt for extragalactic signals coming from FRBs.

My Research

In my current research, I am trying to understand the distribution of Millisecond Pulsars (MSPs) in the Galactic Bulge. Finding the missing population of MSPs in this region can help us constrain the Fermi gamma-ray excess in the region. I also work on pulsar single pulse studies, where I attempt to extract the emission statistics like the flux, energy and wait-times to compare them with FRBs.

💫

Pulsars

My primary research interest lies in studying pulsars, which are compact, fast spinning and highly magnetized neutron stars who emit predominantly in the radio spectrum. The emission physics and stellar evolutionary tracks of these objects are still unconstrained.

🌀

Fast Radio Bursts

FRBs are extremely bright radio sources of extragalactic origin thought to be powered by similar coherent mechanisms as neutron stars. My work focuses on broadband radio observations of repeating FRB sources with the Effelsberg 100-m Radio Telescope.

📡

Galactic Center Science

Characterizing radio light variability from the Milky Way's supermassive black hole and searching for hidden pulsar populations in the Galactic Bulge.
